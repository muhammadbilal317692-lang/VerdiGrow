Why it matters

Organic residues are abundant, but their value is being lost.

Vegetable markets and cold storage facilities near the College of Agriculture generate spoiled produce, fruit residues, banana peels and peduncles, leaves, and other biomass year-round. When unmanaged, this material creates pollution, pest pressure, odor, greenhouse gas emissions, and wasted nutrients.

Problem statement

Farmers still depend heavily on chemical fertilizers while nutrient-rich organic waste is discarded nearby. VerdiGrow closes that gap with a practical circular system built around local waste streams and local agricultural need.

Environmental burden

Improper disposal increases pollution, unpleasant odors, and greenhouse gas release.

Resource inefficiency

Useful biomass remains underutilized instead of being converted into productive agricultural inputs.

Soil dependency challenge

Conventional fertility management remains dominated by chemical inputs rather than regenerative alternatives.

Proposed solution

A controlled waste-to-value compost production system.

VerdiGrow collects nearby organic waste, applies biological conversion and composting processes, then packages stable products for agricultural use. The workflow is designed to be simple, local, and scalable.

Stage 01

Collection

Gather organic residues from vegetable markets and nearby cold storage channels.

Stage 02

Segregation & cleaning

Sort the incoming stream to prepare consistent biodegradable material for treatment.

Stage 03

Composting & maturation

Use controlled decomposition, temperature management, and quality handling to stabilize the biomass.

Stage 04

Screening & packaging

Shred, sieve, store, and package outputs into ready-to-use sustainable products.

Business model

A circular local production loop.

Waste enters from nearby sources, processing creates stable farm inputs, and impact compounds through cleaner surroundings, better soil fertility, and employment generation.

  • Local feedstock from recurring organic waste streams.
  • Biological conversion into agricultural value-added products.
  • Revenue centered on compost and expanded product lines over time.
  • Impact aligned with circular economy and climate-resilient agriculture.
Expected impact

Built to improve environmental and agricultural outcomes together.

VerdiGrow is positioned as more than a composting operation. It is a circular agriculture model that connects waste recovery, soil improvement, and local economic value in one system.

Less waste accumulation

Organic residues are diverted from unmanaged disposal into a productive recovery stream.

Improved soil fertility

Recovered nutrients and organic matter support healthier, more sustainable farming systems.

Lower chemical dependence

Organic and biological alternatives help reduce reliance on conventional chemical fertilizers.

Circular economy growth

New operational roles and local enterprise value can emerge from a stronger waste-to-value chain.
